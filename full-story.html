Systems Engineering Project 2

Food Printing Systems

In this project, I developed an innovative food printing system to improve accessibility for visually impaired individuals. This project showcases my ability to apply systems engineering principles to a real-world problem, demonstrating key skills and innovative thinking.

Objective:

  • Analyze a food industry segment using Systems Engineering principles
  • Develop a working prototype demonstrating key features and functionality

Industry Focus:

Evebot Food-Grade Portable Printpen: A handheld inkjet printer for customizing food surfaces like macarons, cakes, and coffee.

Why It Matters

This project demonstrates:

  • Systems Engineering Application: Ability to analyze, design, and implement complex systems.
  • Innovation: Identifying gaps and creating solutions for real-world problems.
  • Technical Skills: Proficiency in research, prototyping, and integrating new technologies.
  • Inclusivity: Developing accessible solutions for diverse user needs.

Solution Concept

To print braille onto food packaging, we selected binder jetting. This involves depositing liquid binding agents into powder layers to create raised surfaces. This method builds upon the existing Evebot system without needing extensive modifications.

Personal Contributions

  • Research Lead: Investigated food printing systems and accessibility solutions.
  • Concept Development: Selected and developed the binder jetting solution.
  • Prototype Design: Designed and integrated the funnel mechanism with the Evebot system.
