Systems Engineering Project 2

Food printing systems

In this project, I am tasked to develop an innovative food printing system. The output of this project will be to make an improvement to the Evebot food-grade portable system. Although not explicitly stated, we will be constraining the scope of the existing food printing system to the Evebot food-grade portable printpen due to factors such as resource and budget. This project will showcase the use of systems engineering principles and concepts on the food industry topic to explain how we got to each step.

Objective:

  • Selecting a specific food industry segment
  • Analyzing it using Systems Engineering principles
  • Develop a working prototype that demonstrates key features and functionality

Industry :

  • Looking into existing food printers
  • Identifying gaps within existing system
  • Identify ways to improve on it

What is Evebot food-grade portable printpen?

Evebot food-grade portable printpen is a handheld inkjet printer made specifically for food surfaces. The system is designed for customization of food printing, mainly for macarons, cakes, coffee, and more.

Domain exploration

The team and I first researched existing food printing systems, and it landed on the topics of 2D and 3D printers. 2D food printers are essentially traditional inkjet printers, whereas 3D food printers operate through an extruder to move along an X-Y-Z axis.

Functionalities of food printing systems identified:

  • Output materials through the nozzle
  • Move the nozzle in multiple planes
  • Communicate with external devices

Exploration of new ideas

With the current functionalities we gathered, we then started looking into a different domain. The topic we landed is regarding the visually impaired and their issue with accessibility of food information. With the problem identified, we then looked into existing solutions such as braille embossers and Navilens, and functionalities of each solution were identified.

Choosing the solution

The team had decided to pick braille as the project focus, due to reasons such as faster information feedback using tactile and smartphone dependency.

Functionalities of Braille Embossers identified:

  • Elevate the surface
  • Transform input into machine actions
  • Communicate with external devices
  • Move the embossing head across the paper

Comparing and linking functionalities

The functionalities of the food printing system and braille embossers were compared and a link between food printing systems and braille embossers was done which led us to ultimately land into the problem of extruding materials to elevate the surface.

Concept selection

In order to create braille onto food product surfaces, different plausible solutions were looked into such as thermal radiation and ultraviolet light. A comparison between the two did not reach any fruitful conclusion due to their similarities and issues, with the team ultimately sourcing for a different, third alternative.

Binder Jetting

Binder Jetting was the solution that the team ended up with and ultimately created potential concepts to work with. So why binder jetting? Binder jetting involves depositing liquid binding agents into a thin layer of powder particles, which we believe is the most feasible. With the correct amount of liquid and powder, we could potentially achieve an optimal result. Most importantly, unlike the other two solutions which required replacement to be done onto the nozzle, we are able to build upon the existing system.

Concept of solution

In order to print braille onto food packaging, Evebot has to create layers above the surface to elevate it, such that the visually impaired can feel the tactile feedback. Evebot will roll over the surface a few times to output the layers. Powder will be outputted through a funnel that we will be making, whereas liquid particles will be outputted through the original Evebot nozzle.
